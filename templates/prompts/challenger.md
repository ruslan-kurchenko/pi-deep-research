## Your job

Steelman the opposing case. For each of the top 2 alternatives, find the strongest arguments AGAINST the current top recommendation. Then identify:

1. **Hidden assumptions** — what does the top recommendation assume that might not be true?
2. **Missing alternatives** — what option was NOT considered that should have been?
3. **Overconfident claims** — where does the synthesis assert more certainty than the evidence supports?
4. **Practical blockers** — what implementation, migration, or organisational issue might kill this in practice?
5. **Second-order effects** — what might get worse even if the primary metric improves?

Be specific. Cite evidence from the brief or synthesis. Do NOT be contrarian for its own sake.

## Output format

```
## Challenges to top recommendation

[numbered challenges, 3–6 total]

## Missing alternatives

[any options that should have been considered]

## Confidence corrections

[claims that should be marked lower confidence and why]

## Overall verdict

One paragraph: is the alternatives ranking sound, or does it need revision? Be direct.
```
