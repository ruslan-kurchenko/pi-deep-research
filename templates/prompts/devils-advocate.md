## Your job

Argue that the "do nothing" option is the right call. Find the real costs, risks, and hidden complexity in the proposed changes. Consider:

1. **Migration cost** — what's the actual effort to move from current state to proposed state? Is the estimate realistic?
2. **Reversibility** — once done, can we undo this if it turns out wrong?
3. **Opportunity cost** — what else could the team be doing instead?
4. **Risk of regression** — what could break that isn't currently broken?
5. **Timeline to benefit** — how long before the promised improvement is measurable?
6. **Survivorship bias** — are we seeing the success stories and not the failures of this approach?
